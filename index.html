Paid Social Growth Strategy

An actionable plan to reduce acquisition costs, improve customer retention, and scale growth for Petolo through strategic paid social initiatives.

Q1

Diagnosing Meta’s Problem

Key Issues Identified

Audience quality

Low CTR in prospecting suggests relevance issues; retargeting CTR is also below what’s typical for an engaged warm pool.

Creative fit

Current ads may not resonate enough in the first 3 seconds or clearly outline the value.

Funnel friction

Big drop-off from click to policy in prospecting (0.95% CVR vs. 4% Paid Search) suggests mismatch between ad promise and landing page clarity.

Expectation mismatch

Higher churn indicates that people may sign up without fully understanding the coverage or terms.

4-Week, 3-Test Corrective Plan

1
Test 1 – Audience & exclusion structure
  • Separate campaigns for: Advantage+ broad targeting, Lookalikes (6+ month retained customers), Detailed targeting (pet accessories, pet toys, etc.).
  • Exclude high-intent retargeting pools from prospecting to keep audiences pure.
  • Allocate budget dynamically based on early performance.
2
Test 2 – Creative alignment
  • A/B test coverage-first ads (clear price, bullet-point benefits) vs. story-led ads (emotional pet moments with coverage clarity).
  • Ensure first 3 seconds hook with a cost-comparison or clear “protect your pet” message.
3
Test 3 – Funnel optimization
  • Simplify landing page: highlight coverage categories, use claim process visuals, reduce scroll depth for key info.
  • Track if CVR lift correlates with lower churn from these cohorts.

Q2

Expanding Beyond Meta & TikTok

Chosen Platform: Pinterest

Why Pinterest Fits for Petolo:
  • Audience skews toward 25–54-year-old women, a segment often responsible for household pet-related decisions.
  • Strong affinity for pet-related searches: “best dog insurance,” “cat health tips,” “vet visit checklist.”
  • Lower CPMs (€3–€6) than Meta, with high-intent keyword targeting.
  • Planning mindset: users often browse for purchases weeks in advance — aligns with considered products like insurance.
Targeting Structure for €5,000 Test:
  • Prospecting (70%):
    • Interest targeting: Pet care, Dog food, Veterinary services, Pet adoption, Pet accessories.
    • Keyword targeting: “Best pet insurance,” “Dog vet bills,” “Affordable pet insurance,” “Cat surgery cost.”
  • Retargeting (30%):
    • Site visitors, landing page viewers, cart abandoners.
Ad Formats:
  • Video Pins (15–30s) showing vet bill comparisons with and without insurance.
  • Static Pins (1000×1500 px) with coverage icons and price overlays.
  • Carousel to show multiple pet types with one policy.
Initial KPIs:
  • CTR > 1%, CVR aligned to Paid Search benchmark, CAC ≤ €90 in early tests.

Q3

Retention-Focused Acquisition

Audience Approach

  • Lookalikes from customers with >6 months policy retention.
  • Exclude segments that joined via heavy discounts and churned quickly.
  • Include high-intent interests: dog food, veterinary services, pet grooming, premium pet brands.

Creative Adjustments

  • Lead with transparent pricing and “what’s covered vs. not” comparison.
  • Use pet-owner testimonials focusing on claims process ease and real savings.
  • Highlight policy flexibility and multi-pet discounts.

Measurement

  • Tag retention-focused campaigns in CRM for cohort analysis.
  • Track CAC vs. churn rate in first 90 days.
  • Use LTV:CAC ratio to evaluate long-term viability.

Q4

Multi-Pet Owner Campaign

Goal & KPIs

  • Acquire multi-pet owners at CAC ≤ €85, churn ≤20%.
  • KPIs: CAC, CTR, CVR, churn %, LTV.

Targeting

  • Lookalikes of existing multi-pet customers.
  • Interests: multi-pet households, pet care subscriptions, pet accessories, dog food + cat food cross-interest.
  • Retarget visitors who viewed multiple pet profiles or policy pages.

Creative Ideas

  • Carousel: Each card shows a pet and cost saving under one policy.
  • 15s Reel/TikTok: “One plan, two tails, zero stress” — family with two pets visiting vet.
  • Static: Side-by-side of two separate policies vs. one bundled.

Measurement

  • Cohort analysis comparing multi-pet to single-pet LTV and churn.
  • Optimise budget split weekly based on CAC trends.
